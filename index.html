About the Author

Antar Atreya belongs from the beautiful Northeastern state- Amazing Assam!

A graduate in Economics from Ramjas College, University of Delhi; he decided to turn into a storyteller and author instead of following the conventional route of taking up a job. He wrote his first book, "Classic Suspense Stories" at the age of 14 which sold more than 500 copies in 2009. In 2018 he co-authored with Durjoy Datta and other emerging writers in a book "Pocketful O’ Stories: Micro-tales on Unexpected Moments of Love". The book published by Penguin Random House India, features five of his heart-warming nano-tales on love. His latest book is titled, Chronicles of the Mortal Vishnu. It is a mytho-fantasy fiction saga set in the next Treta yuga.

He believes that, everyone can create their own world through stories and to inspire budding writers he has also launched a Youtube segment- Turn into an author with Antar. In this segment he shares the intricate details of 'how to write a book?' and ‘how to get your book published?' A socially responsible Indian, the author will sponsor the education of rescued girl child labor(s) from the profits of his first book!

Book Blurb

THE YEAR IS 3300 AND IT IS THE TRETA YUGA OF THE NEW AGE. "Will God too turn corrupt?"

In the new world after civilization was established and political and economic structures emerged with time and necessity; mankind finally welcomed injustice.
The earth’s first born who had mentored mankind's progress and laid the first civilization turned into the tyrannical Bhuswami, creating the Daitya clan. Bestowed with power from the Gods he became authorative, invincible and ruthless.

With their own stories, two prodigal warriors turn into Maharathis. They had their own journeys of discovering their potential, gaining unparalleled might and realizing the power of LOVE. Supported by his 'destined one', one of the Maharathis manifested himself as A MORTAL DIVINE. The two-gather liberated a river from the locks of Lord Shiva, resurrected kingdoms and slayed a pack of violent wolves. With long lasting political turmoil across the seven landmasses, the world was in the brink of the First Global War. The Daityas gathered a colossal force and facing them was a coalition of Ten Kings. The Ten Kings were brought together with various reasons by the two Maharathis under the insignia of Garuda. Also aiding them were the mystic tribes of Narklokis and Yakshas; for they had made promises in the past.

With the global war knocking; a sudden surprising event creates a question -

"Does God too turns corrupt?"
